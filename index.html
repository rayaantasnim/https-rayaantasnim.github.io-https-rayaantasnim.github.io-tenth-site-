What is a wheel?
The wheel is a circular object that revolves on an axle and is fixed below a vehicle or other object to enable it to move easily over the ground.
Normally, there are 4 wheels in a normal car.
This special car is equipped with twelve uniquely designed wheels that reflect both functionality and artistic flair. Each wheel varies in size and color, arranged symmetrically along the body to ensure balance and stability across all terrains. The wheels are engineered to adapt to road, water, and even underwater environments, with pressure-resistant materials and flexible suspension systems. Their multicolored concentric patterns not only enhance visual appeal but also serve as indicators for rotation speed and terrain feedback. The front wheels are slightly smaller for agile steering, while the rear wheels are larger and reinforced for propulsion and load-bearing. Overall, the twelve wheels are not just mechanical components—they are intelligent, adaptive units that contribute to the car’s versatility, speed, and elegance across land, air, and sea.
